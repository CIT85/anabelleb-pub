Garden design

Bring the outdoors inside with a beautiful, functional indoor herb garden. Whether you're creating a vibrant kitchen garden or a relaxing corner of greenery, thoughtful design can elevate both the aesthetic and health of your plants. Here’s how to design a garden that’s both practical and stylish, no matter your space or style preferences.


Choosing the Right Location

Maximize Sunlight & Accessibility

Where you place your herb garden plays a huge role in its success. Look for a spot with at least 4-6 hours of indirect sunlight. Ideally, this will be near a south-facing window or a space where you can easily place grow lights if needed.

Ensure the space is also easy to access—whether on a kitchen countertop, windowsill, or floating shelf. This will make watering and harvesting much more convenient.


Design Style Ideas

Your herb garden can be more than just functional—it can complement your home's décor. Here are a few design styles to consider:

Rustic/Traditional
Add a cozy, cottage feel with clay pots, wooden crates, or metal containers. These natural materials create a warm, inviting atmosphere.
Modern/Minimalist
Choose sleek, contemporary containers in neutral colors like concrete or metal for a clean, modern look. Keep it simple, yet stylish.
Boho
Create an earthy vibe with woven baskets, terracotta pots, and hanging planters. This relaxed, boho style adds personality and charm.

Plan Your Herb Layout

Smart Arrangement for Growth and Accessibility

Plan how your herbs will grow to ensure they have room to thrive:

  • Tall Herbs (like basil, mint, and rosemary) should go in the back or center of the space.
  • Shorter Herbs (such as parsley, thyme, and chives) can be placed in the front for easy access.
  • Consider grouping herbs with similar light and watering needs together. Vertical planters are a great way to save space in smaller areas.

Add Decorative Touches

Personalize Your Garden

Small design details can make your garden even more enjoyable:

  • Plant Labels: Simple labels help keep your herbs organized while adding a charming touch.
  • Trellis or Plant Supports: For climbing herbs like beans or peas, a small trellis adds both function and beauty.
  • Fairy Lights or Lanterns: Add a cozy ambiance to your herb garden with soft lighting, perfect for evenings.
